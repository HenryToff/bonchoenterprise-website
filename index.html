CONDITIONS WE TREAT.

At Boasark Physiotherapy Clinic, we treat a wide variety of conditions to help you recover and achieve optimal health. Our physiotherapists specialize in treating:

BACKACHE

It is typically caused by muscle strain, poor posture, herniated discs, or conditions like arthritis.

ARTHRITIS

Arthritis is a condition that causes inflammation in the joints, leading to pain, stiffness, and swelling.

SPONDYLOSIS

Spondylosis refers to the degenerative changes in the spine, commonly affecting the vertebrae, discs, and joints due to aging or wear and tear.

FRACTURE

Fractures refer to broken bones, which can occur due to trauma, falls, accidents, or underlying medical conditions that weaken bones.

DISLOCATION

A dislocation occurs when the bones in a joint are forced out of their normal position, often due to trauma, falls, or accidents.

STROKE

A stroke occurs when there is a sudden interruption of blood flow to the brain, causing brain cells to become damaged or die. This can lead to loss of motor function, speech, and cognitive abilities.

FACIAL PALSY

Facial palsy refers to the loss of movement or weakness of the muscles on one side of the face, often resulting from damage to the facial nerve

CEREBRIAL PALSY

Cerebral palsy (CP) is a group of neurological disorders that affect movement, muscle coordination, and posture due to brain damage or abnormal brain development, usually occurring before or during birth

SPINAL CORD INJURY

A spinal cord injury (SCI) is a severe condition resulting from trauma or disease that causes damage to the spinal cord, leading to loss of motor, sensory, or autonomic function.

ERB'S PALSY

Erb's palsy, also known as Erb-Duchenne palsy, is a type of brachial plexus injury that results from damage to the network of nerves controlling the arm and hand, typically occurring during birth.

CLUB FOOT

Clubfoot (also known as talipes equinovarus) is a congenital deformity where a baby’s foot is twisted out of shape or position, typically turning inward and downward.

SPORT INJURY

Cerebral palsy (CP) is a group of neurological disorders that affect movement, muscle coordination, and posture due to brain damage or abnormal brain development, usually occurring before or during birth

PARALYSIS

Paralysis refers to the loss of the ability to move and sometimes feel part of the body, usually due to injury or damage to the nervous system, such as the spinal cord or brain

PARKINSON DISEASE

Parkinson's disease (PD) is a neurodegenerative disorder that primarily affects movement. It is characterized by the gradual degeneration of dopamine-producing neurons in the brain,
