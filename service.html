We Specialise in

ORTHOPAEDIC &
SPORTS PHYSIO

Focuses on diagnosing, treating, and preventing musculoskeletal injuries and conditions. It addresses issues like fractures, sprains, and joint or muscle pain through targeted exercises, manual therapy, and rehabilitation programs. The goal is to restore strength, flexibility, and function, helping individuals return to daily activities or sports safely and efficiently

NEURO-REHABILATION

Neurorehabilitation focuses on restoring function and improving the quality of life for individuals with neurological disorders or injuries, such as stroke, spinal cord injury, or Parkinson's disease. It involves tailored therapies to enhance mobility, coordination, and cognitive skills. The goal is to promote recovery, independence, and adaptation to any lasting impairments

PROSTHETIC &
ORTHOLIC

Prosthetic and Orthotic Physiotherapy focuses on helping individuals adapt to and maximize the use of prosthetic limbs or orthotic devices. This specialized care includes gait training, strength-building exercises, and balance improvement. The goal is to enhance mobility, independence, and overall function, ensuring optimal use and comfort of the prosthesis or orthosis

POST-SURGICAL
PHYSIO

Post-Surgical Physiotherapy focuses on aiding recovery after surgery by restoring mobility, strength, and function. It involves personalized exercises, pain management techniques, and guidance to prevent complications such as stiffness or blood clots. The goal is to promote healing, reduce recovery time, and help patients safely return to daily activities

PAEDIATRIC
REHABILITAION

Paediatric Rehabilitation focuses on enhancing the physical, cognitive, and emotional development of children with disabilities, injuries, or chronic conditions. It involves tailored therapies, such as physiotherapy, occupational therapy, and speech therapy, to improve mobility, strength, coordination, and independence. The goal is to help children reach their fullest potential by addressing developmental delays, neuromuscular disorders, or recovery from surgery or trauma, ensuring they achieve the best possible quality of life.

CARDIORESPIRATION
PHYSIO

Cardiorespiratory Physiotherapy focuses on the assessment, treatment, and management of patients with cardiovascular and respiratory conditions. This specialized field of physiotherapy aims to improve lung function, enhance heart efficiency, and increase overall endurance and mobility. Therapists employ techniques such as airway clearance, breathing exercises, and physical training to help patients recover from illnesses like chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), asthma, heart failure, and post-surgical complications. The goal is to optimize respiratory function, reduce symptoms, and improve the patient's quality of life

FITNESS & WEIGHT
TRAINING (GYM)

Fitness and weight training involve structured exercises to improve strength, endurance, and overall physical health. This includes resistance training with weights or machines and cardiovascular workouts. Regular training enhances muscle tone, boosts metabolism, and supports weight management, contributing to overall well-being and fitness

MASSAGE THERAPY

Massage therapy involves the manipulation of soft tissues in the body to promote relaxation, relieve pain, and improve circulation. It can reduce stress, enhance flexibility, and support overall well-being through various techniques, such as kneading, pressing, and stroking.

DIETARY THERAPY

Dietary therapy involves using specific nutritional strategies to prevent, manage, or treat health conditions. It focuses on tailored diet plans that address individual needs, promoting optimal health and well-being through balanced nutrition. This approach can support recovery, manage chronic diseases, and improve overall quality of life

PAIN MANAGEMENT

Pain management is a multidisciplinary approach to alleviating pain and improving quality of life for individuals suffering from acute or chronic pain. It involves various strategies, including medications, physical therapy, psychological support, and interventional procedures. The goal is to reduce pain levels, enhance functionality, and address the underlying causes, while considering the patient's overall well-being

CONDITIONS WE TREAT.

At Boasark Physiotherapy Clinic, we treat a wide variety of conditions to help you recover and achieve optimal health. Our physiotherapists specialize in treating:

BACKACHE

It is typically caused by muscle strain, poor posture, herniated discs, or conditions like arthritis.

ARTHRITIS

Arthritis is a condition that causes inflammation in the joints, leading to pain, stiffness, and swelling.

SPONDYLOSIS

Spondylosis refers to the degenerative changes in the spine, commonly affecting the vertebrae, discs, and joints due to aging or wear and tear.

FRACTURE

Fractures refer to broken bones, which can occur due to trauma, falls, accidents, or underlying medical conditions that weaken bones.

DISLOCATION

A dislocation occurs when the bones in a joint are forced out of their normal position, often due to trauma, falls, or accidents.

STROKE

A stroke occurs when there is a sudden interruption of blood flow to the brain, causing brain cells to become damaged or die. This can lead to loss of motor function, speech, and cognitive abilities.

FACIAL PALSY

Facial palsy refers to the loss of movement or weakness of the muscles on one side of the face, often resulting from damage to the facial nerve

CEREBRIAL PALSY

Cerebral palsy (CP) is a group of neurological disorders that affect movement, muscle coordination, and posture due to brain damage or abnormal brain development, usually occurring before or during birth

SPINAL CORD INJURY

A spinal cord injury (SCI) is a severe condition resulting from trauma or disease that causes damage to the spinal cord, leading to loss of motor, sensory, or autonomic function.

ERB'S PALSY

Erb's palsy, also known as Erb-Duchenne palsy, is a type of brachial plexus injury that results from damage to the network of nerves controlling the arm and hand, typically occurring during birth.

CLUB FOOT

Clubfoot (also known as talipes equinovarus) is a congenital deformity where a baby’s foot is twisted out of shape or position, typically turning inward and downward.

SPORT INJURY

PARALYSIS

Paralysis refers to the loss of the ability to move and sometimes feel part of the body, usually due to injury or damage to the nervous system, such as the spinal cord or brain
